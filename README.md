# COVID-19 Country-Level Pro-Social Behaviour Analysis

## Overview
 The project investigates country-level predictors of pro-social behaviour during the early COVID-19 pandemic using the PsyCorona dataset, with a focus on **Hungary**.  

**Achievement:** High Distinction (HD) awarded for this assignment.

The analysis explores how social, economic, health, and political factors influence willingness to engage in pro-social behaviours, including donations, helping others, and adherence to health guidelines. Methods include data preprocessing, visualization, clustering, and predictive modeling.

---

## Dataset
- Source: PsyCorona Baseline Study (Van Lissa et al., 2022)  
- Observations: 40,000  
- Variables: 52, covering employment, social contacts, health, life satisfaction, and demographics  
- Focus Country: Hungary  
- The dataset contains both quantitative and categorical responses  

---

## Key Tasks
- **Data Cleaning and Preprocessing:** Handling missing values, encoding categorical variables, and preparing a clean dataset for analysis (pages 5–20).  
- **Exploratory Data Analysis:** Distribution plots for happiness, life satisfaction, boredom, and social contact behaviours (pages 21–30). 
- **Clustering Analysis:** K-means and hierarchical clustering to identify countries with similar patterns to Hungary (pages 77–83).  
- **Predictive Modeling:** Linear regression models to predict pro-social behaviours and willingness to make personal sacrifices during the pandemic, including model summaries and top predictors (pages 45–74, 90–98).  
- **Visualization:** Boxplots and bar charts for comparative analysis of behaviours across countries (pages 25–42).
